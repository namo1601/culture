THE LANGUAGE OF KARNATAKA

Kannada

Kannada is the official language of Karnataka and one of the major languages of India

Family:
Kannada belongs to the Dravidian language family, which also includes languages like Telugu, Tamil, and Malayalam.

Scripts:
It is written in the Kannada script, which has its origins in the Brahmi script and has evolved over centuries.

Cultural Significance:
Kannada has a rich literary tradition, with notable poets and writers like Kuvempu, B. M. Srikantaiah, and D. R. Bendre. It boasts classical literature, modern novels, and a vibrant oral tradition.

Tulu

Tulu is a Dravidian language primarily spoken in the coastal regions of Karnataka, particularly in the districts of Dakshina Kannada and Udupi.

Family:
Tulu belongs to the Dravidian family of languages, which includes Kannada, Tamil, Telugu, and Malayalam.

Scripts:
Tulu is traditionally written in the Tulu script, which is derived from the Brahmi script. However, it is often written in the Kannada script in modern contexts.

Cultural Significance:
Tulu has a rich oral tradition, including folk songs, stories, and rituals that reflect the local culture and traditions. The language is closely associated with the Tulu-speaking community's identity.
